BROWNIE BASICS

Fundamentally, brownies are made of fat, flour, chocolate (solid chocolate or cocoa powder) sugar and a coagulating agent to bind your ingredients together and a leavening agent depending on your brownie type.

What determines your brownie is your fat and the flour ratio.

Since brownies can be made in a short time, there will be no quick & easy version.

  • Texture: Determined by fat (chocolate & butter)
  • Structure: Determined by flour ratio
  • General Method/ Steps when making brownies:
  1. Preheat oven 180°C/ 350°F
  2. Grease then line a suitable baking tin with parchment paper. Remember to leave a slight overhang on all sides to
  3. In a double boiler/ heatproof bowl over a pot of bubbling water, melt butter and chocolate. Stir occasionally until the mixture is smooth and melted.
  4. Whisk in sugar into the slightly warm mixture to ensure the sugar is fully melted.
  5. Allow the bowl to cool on one side or transfer the mixture to another bowl before whisking the eggs one at the time, fully incorporating the egg first before adding in the next egg for easier mixing.
  6. Whisk in ingredients such as cocoa and salt, then fold in the flour mixture until combined. Add in any preferred additions after folding in the flour mixture.
  7. Pour your batter into the pan and bake it until a inserted toothpick/ cake tester comes out with moist crumbs. Allow the brownie to cool in the pan for 15 minutes. Lift the brownies using the overhang parchment and cool the brownie on a wire rack.
  8. Cut your brownie into a desired shape/ portions, then serve!
