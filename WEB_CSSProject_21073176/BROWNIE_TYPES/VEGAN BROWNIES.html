VEGAN BROWNIES

Since the vegan diet does not consist of animal product, finding a vegan recipe for brownies proved to be a surprise.

This recipe creates a brownie that is comparable with a regular brownie, with everything a fudgy brownie lovers wants in a brownie: a fudgy, intensely chocolaty and moist brownie.

  • Prep Time: 15 minutes
  • Cook Time: 35 minutes
  • Total time: 50 minutes
  • Servings: 12 square brownies
    •

Ingredients

  • 8 tablespoons (120 mL) aquafaba (liquid from a can of chickpeas, use unsalted chickpeas)
  • 1 & ½ cups (290g) organic cane sugar or pure cane sugar
  • 6 ounces (170g) 65-75% dark chocolate, roughly chopped
  • ½ cup or 8 tablespoons (112g) vegan butter, cubed
  • 1 amp; ½ cups (180g) all-purpose flour
  • 7 tablespoons (42g) Dutch process cocoa powder
  • ¾ teaspoon fine sea salt
  • 1 tablespoon pure vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon espresso powder
  • 3 ounces (85g) dark or bittersweet chocolate chips, or finely chopped dark chocolate

Method/ Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 180°C/ 350°F. Lightly grease a 8" x 8" pan and line with parchment, ensure there is some overhang on the sides.
  2. Sift the flour and cocoa powder together in a medium bowl.
  3. Add the aquafaba and sugar to a large mixing bowl. Use an electric handheld mixer or a stand mixer on high speed and beat the mixture for 2 ½ to 3 minutes until thickened, glossy, and wavy ribbons frequently appear in the bowl.
  4. Add in the vanilla, salt, and espresso powder, and fold with a silicone spatula until well combined (the espresso will continue dissolving into the mixture as it rests).
  5. Melt the chopped dark chocolate and cubed vegan butter in a water bath/ double boiler, allow the mixture to melt while stirring occasionally until fully incorporated.
  6. Pour the warm butter-chocolate mixture over the aquafaba-sugar mixture and fold together with the silicone spatula.
  7. Add the flour-cocoa mixture to the wet ingredients and stir until just combined (stop mixing once the flour traces are gone). Fold in the chocolate chips. Pour the batter into the lined pan and smooth out the surface with the spatula.
  8. Bake the brownies in the preheated oven for 34 to 37 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted in the middle comes out with some moist crumbs and/or a tiny bit of thick batter (the latter could be the melted chocolate chips). If the batter is liquidy or runny, the brownies need about 5 more minutes in the oven.
  9. Transfer the brownies to a wire rack to cool for 30 minutes. Then carefully lift the brownies out of the pan using the parchment paper handles onto the wire rack. Allow to cool for a further 20 to 30 minutes before slicing. Slice the brownies and enjoy!
