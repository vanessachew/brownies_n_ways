BROWNIES N WAYS

TIPS:

  • BASICS:
  1. Measure accurately. Baking is as much science as art, if not more so. Invest in a digital scale and an oven thermometer for the best results.
  2. When melting chocolate & butter, ensure the mixture is not exposed to any liquid.
  3. Before adding eggs to the butter & choco mixture, remember to cool the mixture down. If not, you will get scrambeled eggs in your batter!

  4. While adding in eggs into a hot mixture is definitly a horrible idea, adding sugar into a hot mixture is great!
    • This is a great way to melt your sugar and avoid having sugar crystals in your baked brownie, hence allowing the brownie to have an even distribution of sugar.

  5. When whisking in eggs, remember to patient and fully whisk and mix fully before adding in the next egg. This will allow your batter to come together faster and more efficiently.
  6. Cocoa powder is an optional ingredient when baking brownies, but when included, your brownies will have an extra rich and intense flavour.
    • Cocoa powder is also a key ingredient to making rich and chewy brownies!
    • There are also differences within types of cocoa powder. All the types are just a product of different methods use to process cocoa. There are 2 types, dutch-process and natural.

      • Dutch-Process: washed in alkaline soluton of potassium carbonate. This neutralises the cocoa beans' acidity, causing it to be neutral, Dutch-process cocoa powder is unable to react with baking soda, so add in baking powder instead! Since Dutch-processed cocoa powder dampens sharp flavours so when baking, remember to get a good brand.
      • Natural: acidic and bitter, with a very strong and concentrated chocolate flavour. As it is acidic, most recipes use baking soda to allow the combo to act as a leavener.

  7. Folding is a technique that is often used when you don't want to overwork your batter and deflate the air in your batter. If your batter is overworked, you will have a batter with too much gluten. This will affect the structure of your brownie, giving it a heavy, bready texture.
  8. Additions/ toppings such as nuts, chocolate pieces or fruits of your preference may be added for an extra boost in the texture/ flavour for the brownie.
  9. The bake time may vary depending on your type of brownie and oven. The general method is using a toothpick to test if the brownie is ready. Generally, brownies are ok if they're slightly underbaked, and it is much better to underbake your brownies than to overbake them, because you may obtain a dry brownie instead.
  • CAKEY BROWNIES:
  1. When making cakey brownies, include baking powder & Dutch-processed cocoa powder.
  2. Be sure to let the brownies cool before you cut and serve them. When they first come out of the oven they'll be like chocolate cake; but as they cool they settle, and "cake" becomes "dense cake" â€” the perfect cake-type brownie.
  • CHEWY BROWNIES:
  1. To get a chewier brownie, add in brown sugar instead of granulated sugar
  • FUDGY BROWNIES:
  1. If your fudgy brownie doesn't have the perfect level of 'fudginess', add more butter and chocolate gradually. Alternatively, decrease the flour ratio
  • KETO BROWNIES:
  1. For this recipe, using dutch processed cocoa is recommended as the recipe mimics boxed brownie.
  2. However, this can be substituted by using only 1/4 cup of unsweetened regular cocoa powder.
  3. Preferrably, use granulated sweeteners such as erythritol or monk fruit sweetener, but using regular sugar is fine too!
  • VEGAN BROWNIES:
  1. Coffee enhances the chocolatiness in chocolate and deepens the rich flavor (without tasting like coffee). If you don't have espresso powder, you can substitute with instant coffee powder (use slightly more, about 1 & 1/4 to 1 & 1/2 teaspoons).
  2. Since vegan diets do not include eggs, your choco-butter mixture does not need to be cooled too much. Mixing in the rest of the ingredients to a slightly warmer/ higher temperature mixture will not cook anything.
