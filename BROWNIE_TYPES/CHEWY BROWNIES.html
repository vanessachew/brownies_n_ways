BROWNIES N WAYS

CHEWY BROWNIES

A chewy brownie is the best of both worlds: an in-between of a cakey and a fudgy brownie. This recipe makes 16 pieces of moist, indulgent, rich and chewy brownies.

Brownies are chewy when they're high in sugar, fat, and eggs but low in flour. It is somewhat similar to a fudgy brownie, but with a drastically different texture.

Technically, you can make a fudgy brownie using this recipe, as the fat-and-flour ratio used by fudgy brownies and chewy brownies are similar. The only difference is the sugar. Substituting brown sugar with granulated sugar when making brownies will somewhat allow you to procure a batch of fudgy brownies.

  • Prep Time: 20 minutes
  • Cook Time: 40 minutes
  • Total time: 60 minutes
  • Servings: 9 square brownies
    •

Ingredients

  • 8 tablespoons unsalted butter, plus more for pan (113.4g)
  • 8 ounces semi-sweet chocolate bar, coarsely chopped (226.8g)
  • 7/8 cups brown sugar (190g)
  • 3 large eggs, at room temperature (roughly 171g)
  • 1/4 cup unsweetened cocoa powder (32g)
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • ½ cup all-purpose flour, plus 2 tablespoons (64g)
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract
  • ½ cup semi-sweet chocolate chips (64g) [optional]

Method/ Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 180 degrees C/ 350 degrees F. Lightly grease a 8" x 8" baking pan with cold butter and line with parchment paper on all sides. Make sure to leave some parchment overhanging on all 4 sides.
  2. In a bowl set on top of a pot with simmering water, melt the butter and chopped chocolate bar. Using a rubber spatula, stir until the mixture is melted and smooth. Turn off the heat. Set the bowl aside and gradually whisk in the sugar until nicely melted.
  3. Allow the bowl to cool on one side or transfer the mixture to another bowl. Whisk in eggs one at a time, then whisk in the vanilla extract. Mix in the cocoa and salt until the batter is smooth.
  4. Fold in the flour, scraping the sides, until fully combined. Then, fold in chocolate chips [optional].
  5. Pour the batter into the prepared pan and gently smooth out the surface.
  6. Bake for about 38-40 minutes, until set and a cake tester or toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean (a few moist crumbs in the tester is fine). Lift brownies from pan. Remove the parchment paper and let cool on a wire rack.
  7. Cut into squares and enjoy!
